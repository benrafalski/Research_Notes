Samantha Ip, et al. [1]

Methodology
  • Study type: Cohort study
  • Objective: cardiovascular events after vaccination
  • Sample size: 46 million adults (population of England)
  • Cofounders: adjusted for age, sex, ethnic group, previous medical history, previous COVID infection
Results
  • Myocarditis:
  • Pericarditis:
  • Thrombocytopenia:
  • Haemorrhage (stroke):

  • Acute myocardial infraction (heart attack):
  • Ischaemic stroke:
  • Composite venous:
  • Pulmonary embolism:
  • Lower limb deep venous thrombosis:
  • Conclusions: shows there are not increase in cardiovascular events following vaccination except the rare events already known (myocarditis/pericarditis)
  • Note: shows statistically significant results for previously known myocarditis/pericarditis and thrombosis; showing that the methodology is sound
  • Note: if the data line crosses the horizontal "1" line then the results are statistically significant
