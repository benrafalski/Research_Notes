Origins

What to think

  • Perfectly reasonable to have originally thought (Early 2020) that lab leak was plausable given it originated in Wuhan and there is a lab in Wuhan
  • However, since then we have a mountain of evidence for a natural origin with the epicenter of the outbreak originating at the Huanan seafood market in Wuhan
  • Also, Wuhan has lots of rural area with a large bat population, making a bat-to-animal-to-human slip is more likely
  • All the big scientists (Worobey, Andersen, Holmes) all originally thought it was a lab leak at the outbreak but changed their mind when presented with new evidence, that is the scientific process

Lab Leak Theory

RaTG13

  • Shi Zhengli lab had RaTG13 and 8 other SARS related viruses her lab collected from a mine in Mojang. [1]
  • Shi Zhengli published a Nature paper about RaTG13 but didn't release the genomes of the other 8 viruses, leading many to think she was purposefully hiding them. [1]
  • According to Michael Worobey, the 8 other SARS related viruses (later be known as RaTG15) were not a smoking gun and not more closely related to SARS-CoV-2 than RaTG13. [1]
  • According to Eddie Holmes, there was an unpublished paper in 2018 with some sequences related to RaTG13, these sequences had a arbitrary embargo to release of 4 years, so they were released in July 2022 and none of them resembled SARS-CoV-2, however they were subject to many conspiracies because they were taken down after being released. Two Lineages

  • There were two different lineages found (A and B) at the wet market at the time of the outbreak, which differ at two different sites across their 30,000 nucleotides, suggesting that were two spillovers into humans so there would have needed to be two different lab researchers that both happened to go to the wet market after being infected for the lab leak theory to be true.
