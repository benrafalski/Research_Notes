Opium in the Strats Settlements

  • After 1867, opium began produce a signaificant amount of revenue for the Straits Settlements
  • Opium was imported from India in bulk to be sold to the Governemnt of China
  • The Chinese merchants in Malaya would then be able to sell it
  • The Chinese merchants were often wealthy Chinese who had enough money to outbid everyone else trying to buy and sell opium
  • This supplied the British government with almost half of their revenue, by 1904 it was 59% of revenue
  • The government also sanctioned alchohol during this time as well.
