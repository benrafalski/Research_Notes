Malaysian Islamc Party formed

  • As Malay nationalism began to rise, the Islamic Malaysians were represented by Kaum Muda
  • In Match 1947, the Supreme Religious Council of Malaysia (MATA) was fomed at a Pan-Malayan Islamic confrence organized by the PKMM
  • By March 1948, the first Islamic political party in the fight for independence was formed called Hizbul Muslimin
  • Hizbul Muslimin's main goal was an independent Malaysian Islamic State
  • However, Hizbul Muslimin was dissolved later that year during the Malayan Emergency after the British banned them for allegedly having relations with the Communist Party of Malaya
  • By 1951, the UMNO had a growing desire to form a separate Islamic political organization
  • This led to the formation of the Malaysian Islamic Party (PAS)
  • Many PAS members were also dual UMNO members as well
