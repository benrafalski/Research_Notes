Malayan Emergency

  • The Malayan emergency was a period of unrest following the creation of the Federation of Malaya
  • In 1930, the Malayan Communinst Party was formed
  • They were mostly Chinese and wanted an independent communist Malaya
  • After the federation was formed, in April, they went into the jungles and began a guerrilla insurgency against the colonial government
  • In June 1948, the government declared a state of emergency after 3 European planters were murdered by communists in Perak
  • The majority of the Chinese did not back the communists
  • The British responed with military suppression and forcibly moved many rural Chinese into strategically controlled "New Villages" to cut off food for the guerrillas
  • This isolated villagers from guerrillas but resulted in increased government unpopularity
  • Under High commissioner Sir Gerald Templer, the British finally started to ease some of the social/political tensions
  • Templer implemented local elections and the creation of village councils, facilitating independence
  • He also gave many Chinese citizenship
  • These measures decreased the support for the insurgency in the mid-1950s
  • By July 1960, the emergency was officially declared over
