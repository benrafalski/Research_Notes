Liu Shan Bang Revolt

  • In February 1857, Liu Shan Bang led 600 Chinese rebels to revolt on James Brooke
  • Brooke fled accross the Kuching River while the rebels burnt his house down
  • The rebels mistook a 17-year old boy as Brooke and beheaded him then put his head on a pole
  • However, the rebels only controlled Kuching for a day.
  • Brooke and his nephew Charles Brooke led a force of Bidayuh and Iban soldiers and crushed the rebels.
  • Charle's Iban forces slaughtered 3,000 villagers in the process.
