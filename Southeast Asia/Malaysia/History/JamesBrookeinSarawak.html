James Brooke in Sarawak

  • In 1839, James Brooke, an English adventurer helped the chief minister of the sultan of Brunei, Pengiran Muda Hassim, suppress a local Iban revolt from Sarawak
  • In 1841, Brooke was made raja (governor) of the Sarawak river basin
  • In 1846, the Brunei sultan himself confirmed Brooke as raja
  • Brooke established a government and made imperial expeditions accross Sarawak over the next 17 years
  • In 1863, he returned to England and then died in 1868
  • He left his nephew to succeed him.
  • In 1868, Brooke consolidated his control of the surrounding districts
