Kesatuan Melayu Muda founded

  • Formed in 1938 in Kuala Lumpur, by a group of leftist Malay intelligentsia educated in Sultan Idris Training College
  • Was the first nationalist movement in Malaysia at a time when nationalism began to rise
  • Nationalism began to rise due to Malaysia becoming more educated and the Chinese/Indians not integrating with them
  • They had a notable willingness to use violence to meet their ends
  • The KMM was always radical and never gained more than 60 members support
  • The KMM believed in radical anti-colonialism that had spread from Indonesia
  • They led several Malay Congresses from 1939-1941 until the Japanese occupation
  • After the Japanese invaded, they ended up fighting alongside them thinking they would help give them independence.
  • However, the Japanese rejected the KMM request for an independent state
