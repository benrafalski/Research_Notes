Malayan Union proposal

  • In April 1946, the Malayan Union was proposed by the British to replace British Malaya
  • The proposal was approved by the Malay rulers, however reluctant to cede their power to the British
  • The Malayan Union was a single state that would hold all the Malayan territories (all of peninsular Malaysia) except Singapore
  • The Union would afford equal rights and citizenship to all ethnicities, including non-Malays, and be administered by a British government
  • This resulted in an upsurge of Malay nationalism and outrage in opposition to the plan
  • Eventually, a Malay politician Dato Onn bin Jaafar formed the UMNO against the Malayan Union
  • Malays also wore white headbands to symbolize their opposition
  • After years of strikes, demonstrations, and boycotts, the Malayan Union was dissolved and resplaced by the Federation of Malaya
