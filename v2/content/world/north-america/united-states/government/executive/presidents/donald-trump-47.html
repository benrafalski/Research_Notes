  • Autism: RFK Jr has claimed that autism is an "epidemic" and that we know that it is caused by "environmental exposure." [1] The autism rate in 8 year olds in the US was 3.2% (1 in 31) in 2022, up from 0.66% in 2000. Research suggests 80% of autism risk is genetic, including Fragile X syndrome, tuberous sclerosis complex, Phelan-McDermid syndrome, and Prader-Willi syndrome. In the late 1990s, British researcher Andrew Wakefield published a since-debunked study linking measles vaccines to higher autism rates, RFK Jr has since promoted the theory that vaccines cause autism. No rigorous studies have found links between autism and vaccines or their components such as thimerosal or formaldehyde. Also, vaccination rates have declined as autism rates have climbed. Researchers largely attribute the rise in autism diagnoses to more widespread screening and the inclusion of a wider range of behaviors to describe the condition. Historically, the definition of autism included moderate to severe intellectual disabilities but today the most severe forms account for only about 25% of cases. [2] Despite Kennedy saying they would go after autism, during the first 4 months of 2025, has reduced funding for autism-related research by an estimated $31 million to $116 million from $147 million in the same period in 2024. The funding cuts came because they involved diverse populations, studied differences in gender or took place at research universities currently under scrutiny by the Trump Admin. [3] In June 2025, a review on thimerosal and autism that was due to be presented by anti-vaxxer Lyn Redwood cited a study that didn't exist and had the opposite conclusion of a study posted on the CDC website two days before that found no link between thimerosal and autism.
