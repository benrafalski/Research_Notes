  • Trump fraud case On August 21, 2025, a New York state appeals court threw out the half-billion-dollar penalty while preserving a fraud case against Trump, enabling him to rebound from one of his biggest legal defeats. The court ruled 4-1 to let the fraud finding stand, but all found problems in the judge's handling of the case and two would have ordered a new trial. A fifth judge would have dismissed the case altogether, and all five judges would have voided the payout. Justice Peter Moulton and chief judge Dianne Renwick agreed Trump "engaged in a decade-long pattern of financial fraud and illegality" but also said Trump's wrongdoing did not cause "cataclysmic harm" to justify a half-billion-dollar award.
