Death Count

  • Unreliability of Gaza health ministry data:
    • Old method: GHM used to certify each death using a collection system from hospitals and morgues during the first month of the war. This collection system was relatively accurate in the past and lined up with UNRWA death counts during Oct 7 - Nov 10.
    • New method: When hospitals began to shut down, on Nov. 10 the GHM started to also use a new method: "reliable media reports". Accounts for more than 14,000/32,000 reported deaths. Much more difficult to verify when compared to the central collection system.
    • Note when viewing: At the start of November, most hospitals were closed. Thus, it is expected to see the death count for central collection to decrease at the start of November.

  • Israeli numbers: IDF claims 13,000 Hamas militants have been killed during the war
  • May 7:
    • GHM changes changes overall death toll
    • Still says 35,000 are dead but says 21,000 have been bodies confirmed at hospitals, 3,700 are dead reported online with identity numbers, and 10,000 are having "incomplete data" (bodies with no identity number or name)
    • Estimates 10,000 are dead under rubble
    • Previous estimates had women and children dead at over 70% of the total, revised numbers changed to 60%
    • WHO says previous robust GHM system of calculating death numbers was credible
    • Lancet peer-reviewed article in Lancet says that first 7,000 deaths were not likely fabricated
    • Hamas control of the GHM is hard to assess due to IDF occupation in most of Gaza
    • Netanyahu says he believes 14,000 Hamas militants have been killed and 16,000 civilians, Hamas says those numbers are exaggerated but did not provide their own death toll
  • Sources: [1] [2] [3] [4]

Gaza Blockade

  • Claims: "Israel banned the import of books, candles, crayons, clothing, cups, cutlery, crockery, electric appliances such as refrigerators and washing machines, glasses, light bulbs, matches, musical instruments, needles, sheets, blankets, shoes, mattresses, spare machine and car parts, and thread"
  • This claim seems to lead back to the same Gisha report where they just interviewed Gazans and humanitarian groups who said they "deduced" if items had been banned from their experience with item imports and from an Amira Hass Haaretz article that references Gazan merchants and human rights activists
  • Israel says they actually do not even have a banned list, items are allowed or rejected based on "who has packaged the products and where do they come from"
  • June 17, 2010: Israel agrees to ease restrictions on items permitted into Gaza Strip after the Free Gaza Movement flottila controversy
  • Full list of items released on July 4, 2010: arms/munitions, dual-use goods, and construction materials.
  • So basically they moved from approving based on where the goods came from, to having a list of specifically prohibited goods that might have military applications
  • Construciton materials: before June 2010 cement and steel were both banned because of the tunnels and after June 2010 they were allowed under international supervision
  • 2744/09 Gisha v. Ministry of Defense (March 22, 2011): Gisha demanded MoD disclose documents (including Red Lines doc, which Israel refused to release because it was a draft) under FOIA that guided policy on letting food into Gaza blockade
  • Red Lines: MoD document that calculated how many trucks of goods needed to enter Gaza to keep the average Gazan calorie intake at 2,279 calories per day (varies by age)
