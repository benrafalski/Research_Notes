AIPAC

Death Count

  • Unreliability of Gaza health ministry data:
    • Old method: GHM used to certify each death using a collection system from hospitals and morgues during the first month of the war. This collection system was relatively accurate in the past and lined up with UNRWA death counts during Oct 7 - Nov 10.
    • New method: When hospitals began to shut down, on Nov. 10 the GHM started to also use a new method: "reliable media reports". Accounts for more than 14,000/32,000 reported deaths. Much more difficult to verify when compared to the central collection system.
    • Note when viewing: At the start of November, most hospitals were closed. Thus, it is expected to see the death count for central collection to decrease at the start of November.

  • Israeli numbers: IDF claims 13,000 Hamas militants have been killed during the war
  • May 7:
    • GHM changes changes overall death toll

IHL Rule 14: Proportionality in Attack [1]

  • "Launching an attack which may be expected to cause incidental loss of civilian life, injury to civilians, damage to civilian objects, or a combination thereof, which would be excessive in relation to the concrete and direct military advantage anticipated, is prohibited."
  • Applicable: both international and non-international armed conflicts.

June 19, 2024 UNCHR Report [1] [2]

  • Quotes:
    1. "This thematic report documents concerns regarding Israel's use of explosive weapons with wide area effects in densely populated areas... particularly in relation to the principles of distinction, proportionality and precautions in attack"
    2. "It details six individual incidents from 7 October to 2 December 2023"
    3. "87 per cent of the verified fatalities have occurred in incidents that resulted in 5 or more fatalities, and over 60 per cent were killed in incidents that resulted in 10 or more fatalities."
    4. "OHCHR calls on Israel to ensure full investigations into the specific incidents cited in this report ... Israel stated that, except for the strike dated to 9 October on Jabalya market, all strikes mentioned in the report were previously referred to the IDF General Staff's Fact-Finding and Assessment Mechanism and were currently being examined before submission to the Military Advocate General (MAG) corps for a decision on whether to open a criminal investigation ... OHCHR remains concerned with the dual role of the MAG, as both legal adviser to the Chief of General Staff and other military authorities and supervisor of disciplinary and criminal investigations. This compromises the independence and impartiality of the investigative system, since the MAG is responsible for investigating violations carried out in operations for which the MAG provided legal advice"
  • Claims:
    1. Principle of distinction: UN claims IDF fails this because they didn't give responses for three of the incidents and that they consider de facto civilian administration and Hamas political structures as military targets
    2. Prohibition of indiscriminate attacks: UN claims IDF fails this becasue they used GBU-31, 32, and 39 munitions and using them (wide area effect munitions) in densely populated areas can be nothing but indiscriminate
    3. The principle of proportionality: UN claims IDF fails this because they use wide area effect bombs and the ones the IDF made no response to seem to be so large that there couldn't possibly be a proportional military advantage and the ones the IDF did respond to seem to be disproportionate with the given information.
    4. The principle of precautions in attack: UN claims IDF fails this because they didn't warn in any of the incidents other than Ash Shujai'yeh. The UN does not accept the leaflets dropped by the IDF to evacuate as sufficient warning.
