July 31, 2024

Ismail Haniyeh killed

  • Hamas leader Ismail Haniyeh assassinated in Tehran in airstrike, confirmed by IRCG and Hamas
  • Haniyeh was attending swearing in of new Iranian president
  • Khaled Meshaal, Haniyeh's deputy-in-exile who lives in Qatar, most likely to succeed Haniyeh
  • Israel, Netanyahu declines to comment on Haniyeh death
  • Blinken says US was not involved in killing
  • Time and place of assassination were chosen to embarrass Tehran, analysts and officials say
  • Haniyeh death sets back chances of imminent ceasefire agreement
  • Hamas armed wing says: "take the battle to new dimensions and have major repercussions"
  • Hamas al-Aqsa TV: "cowardly act that will not go unpunished"
  • Iran declared three days of mourning, vows to retaliate, says US is responsible for supporting Israel
  • Qatar and Egypt both condemn the assassination, says it prevents a ceasefire
  • China, Russia, Turkey, Iraq also condemn assassination, Russia calls for middle east to step back from major war
  • Gazans fear assassination with prolong Gaza war
  • Israeli media has mood of satisfaction following news of assassination
  • Pals in WB condemn Haniyeh killing, say it will have little effect on fight against Israel

Israel strike in Beirut

  • Israel, Gallant says most senior Hezbollah commander, Fuad Shukr, killed in strike in Beirut
  • Hezbollah says Shukr was in the building, did not confirm his fate
  • Strike also kills 3 civilians
  • Strike was retaliation for Golan Heights rocket attack that killed 12
  • IDF considered the Beirut strike as concluding the response to the Golan Heights attack
  • Gallant says Israel is not trying to escalate war but is prepared for all scenarios
  • US defense secretary, Lloyd Austin, says wider war not 'inevitable'

  • 85 sick and severely injured Pals from Gaza, including 35 children evacuated to Abu Dhabi in UAE, WHO operation
