The Buyids

  • The three sons of Buyeh: Imad al-Dawlah, Rukn al-Dawlah, and Mu’izz al-Dawlah founded the Buyid dynasty.
  • Starting in 930, the three brothers captured a majority of Iran
  • in 945, Mu'izz captured Baghdad and all of Iraq
  • He turned the Abbasid caliphate into his puppet, de facto establishing Buyid rule.
  • The Buyids contributed to the Iranian renaissance along with the Samanids.
  • They were a Shi'i Islamic dynasty with strong Iranian culture but chose to maintain the Sunni caliphate.
  • From 1030-1040 the Seljuqs began to grow in power and pushed the Ghaznavids out of Iran.
  • Eventually in 1055, the Seljuqs marched on Baghdad and ended Buyid control and the “Iranian intermezzo” era.
