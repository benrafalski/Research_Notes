The Saffarids

  • In 866, Ya'qub took Sistan and eventually seized Fars and Khuzestan by 869.
  • In 873, he took over Khorasan and overthrew the Tahirids.
  • Ya'qub's army was made up of Kharijites and men from Sistan and he had legitimacy with the caliphate for spreading Islam in Afghanistan and India.
  • In 873, Ya'qub marched on Baghdad to overthrow the caliph but was defeated.
  • In 879 he died and the caliph reluctantly made his brother, Amr ibn Layth, the governor of Khorasan, Fars, and Sistan.
  • Ya'qub will be remembered for leading the Iranian revival.
