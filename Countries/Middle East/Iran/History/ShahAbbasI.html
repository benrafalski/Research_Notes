Shah Abbas I

  • In 1524 Isma'il died and his son, Tahmasp, became Shah.
  • He fought the Ottomans for a while before signing a peace treaty with them in 1555
  • He retreated from public affairs following that
  • In 1588 Abbas became Shah.
  • Having conflicts on both sides from the Ottomans in the west and Uzbeks in the east, he signed a peace treaty with the Ottomans in 1589
  • This ceded land to the Ottomans in northwest Persia, and he focused on the Uzbeks.
  • However, he failed in offensives for 10 years and ended up losing land to both the Uzbeks and India because of his decision to create a standing army.
  • Eventually, in 1598, he succeeded against the Uzbeks and took Khorasan.
  • In 1602 he successfully attacked the Ottomans and recovered the territory he gave to them in the peace treaty.
  • After these victories, Abbas made Esfahan the capital of Iran
  • He turned it into one of the most beautiful cities in the world with sites such as Masjed-e Emam.
