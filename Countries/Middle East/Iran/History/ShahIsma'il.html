Shah Isma'il

  • In the 1480's Shaykh Haydar founded the Safavid movement
  • The Safavids were an extreme Shi'i movement against the Ak Koyunlu.
  • Haydar was eventually killed in battle with Ak Koyunlu
  • In 1501, Isma'il revived the Safavids in northwestern Iran
  • He quickly conquested the rest of Iran and parts of Iraq, Turkey, and Uzbekistan, establishing the Safavids as a dynasty.
  • Under Ismail's rule Iran was fully converted to Twelver Shi'ism.
  • In 1514, the Ottomans, who were Sunni, attacked the Safavids and took over the Safavid capital but withdrew after a mutiny among the Ottoman troops.
  • Following this, Isma'il slowed his expansion and focused more domestically.
  • However, the Ottoman conflict still continued with various skirmishes.
