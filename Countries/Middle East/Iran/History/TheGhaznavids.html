The Ghaznavids

  • In 977, the Samanids made Sebuktigin the governor of Ghazni in Afghanistan, who established the Ghaznavid dynasty.
  • Subuktigin eventually rejected the Samanids.
  • In 997 Sebuktigin died and was succeeded by his son, Mahmud, who took control of Khorasan from the Samanids.
  • Mahmud was staunchly Sunni and gave his allegiance to the Abbasid caliph, who recognized him in return, encouraging him on his conquests.
