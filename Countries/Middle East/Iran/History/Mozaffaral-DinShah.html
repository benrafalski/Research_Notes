Mozaffar al-Din Shah

  • Mozaffar was weak and an incompetent leader who was not prepared to take the throne.
  • He faced a financial crisis so he got unpopular loans from Russia in exchange for political concessions.
  • Mozaffar then made extravagant trips to Europe from 1900-05 with the loan money.
  • In 1901, Mozaffar then made an oil concession to Willian D'Arcy that gave him exclusive rights to Iran's oil fields for 60-years.
  • Under Mozaffar al-Din the mujtahids began to become more involved in government and Shi'i merchants and landowners began to call for political and legal reforms.
  • His corruption and failure to control the financial crisis eventually brought a demand for a new constitution in Iran in 1906.
