Constitutional revolution

  • The growing influence of the West in Iranian education sparked interest in democratic institutions.
  • Between 1905-06 there were multiple protests over government beatings that occurred in Tehran.
  • Eventually, in August 1906, Mozaffar al-Din was forced to grant the demonstrators a constitution.
  • In October, the Majles was opened and the Belgium modeled constitution provided a framework
  • The framework provided for secular legislation, a new judicial code, and free press, placing more power with the Majles and less power with the clergy and throne.
  • The Majles took a strong stance against European intervention.

Education Reforms

  • From 1905-1907, a nationwide, public, primary school system was proposed and approx 3,300 schools were established by the end of the Qajar dynasty.
  • Under the new Majles, they also introduced new reforms that established secular schools and schools for girls.
  • This caused increased tension between the clergy and the Majles.
