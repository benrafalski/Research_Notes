William Morgan Shuster

  • In 1910 the Majles appealed to the US for help reorganizing their financial system
  • Pres. Taft recommended Shuster to head a group of financial experts to head to Iran.
  • In May 1911, Shuster erected bold methods to collect revenue throughout the country.
  • This angered the British and Russians who had economic interests and their respective spheres of influence from the Anglo-Russian Entente.
  • The Russians gave Iran an ultimatum to dismiss Shuster but the Majles refused.
  • The Russians responded by marching on Tehran and Ahmad Shah hastily dismissed Shuster and dissolved the Majles in December.
