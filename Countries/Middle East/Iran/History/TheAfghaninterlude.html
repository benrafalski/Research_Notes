The Afghan interlude

  • After Abbas died his sons fought for power and the Safavids fell into decline.
  • By 1694 Husayn became shah, he was weak and unfit to be a leader.
  • After he took over the Ottomans and Russians took territory from him.
  • The Safavid dynasty would continue to decline until 1720 when Afghans led by Mahmud began to invade Iran.
  • By 1722, they had taken Esfahan and killed Husayn.
  • This marked the effective end of the Safavid dynasty after Mahmud proclaimed himself ruler of Iran.
  • Tahmasp was the next shah of the Safavids and controlled some territory in northern Iran.
  • In 1725, Mahmud was succeeded by his cousin Ashraf.
  • In 1727, the leader of the Ashraf tribe, Nader Khan, attacked the Afghans and expelled them from Iran
  • After that, Tahmasp made him governor of east Iran.
