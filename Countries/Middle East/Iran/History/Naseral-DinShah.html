Naser al-Din Shah

  • Naser al-Din came in as a reformist but became conservative over time.
  • He tried to take back Afghanistan in 1856 but was stopped by the British.
  • He introduced the telegraph, built roads, opened schools, and made Iran's first newspaper.
  • These introductions brought western science, tech, and education into Iran, beginning the modernization of Iran.
  • During this time, Iran was a semicolonial country of the imperial powers
  • Naser al-Din's increasing corruption gave rise to protests that were rallied by a growing power in the form of the Shi'i clergy
  • In 1896, he was assassinated by a Muslim extremist and he was replaced by his son Mozaffar al-Din.
