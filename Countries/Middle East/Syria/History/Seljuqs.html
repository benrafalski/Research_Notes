Seljuqs

  • The Seljuqs were a Turkish people who were Sunni and originally from Khorosan in west Iran
  • After 1063, the Seljuqs expanded and took over all of Iran, Mesopotamia, Syria, and Palestine
  • In 1092, their leader Malik Shah died and the Seljuq empire fell into decline
  • In 1097, they were driven from Syria by the Crusaders
  • By 1124, the Crusaders took Antioch, Jerusalem, parts of Transjordan, and the Syrian coast
  • By 1230, the all Seljuq rule was ended.
