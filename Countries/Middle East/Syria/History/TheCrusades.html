The Crusades

  • The Crusades was a military expedition by the European Christans in the Middle East
  • They were a response to the centuries of Muslim wars of expansion
  • The Crusader's goal was to halt the spread of Islam and retake the Holy Land in the eastern Mediterranean coast
  • After the expansion of Islam, Christanity had been driven from Palestine, Syria, Egypt, and Anatolia
  • In 1095, the first Crusade began and resulted in the founding of a Christian state in Palestine and Syria
  • The Crusaders then split their conquested land into four states, with the king of Jerusalem as administrator
  • By 1128, the Turkish Muslim ruler of Mosul, Zangi, recovered Aleppo from the Crusaders
  • In 1144, he also reclaimed Edessa (southern Turkey) too
  • In 1154, his son, Nur al-Din, would reclaim all of Damascus and later all of Syria
  • After Nur al-Din died, Saladin took over rule, ended the Fatimid caliphate, and founded the Ayyubid dynasty
  • In 1187, Saladin defended against the Crusaders in the Battle of Hattin and resulted in the Muslim reconquest of Jerusalem and all of Palestine
  • In 1189, the Christains mounted the third Crusades and briefly reclaimed part of the Mediterranean coast
  • However, the Crusaders failed in their goal to retake Jerusalem
