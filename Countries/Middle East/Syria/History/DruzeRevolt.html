Great Syrian Revolt

Jabal al-Druze Revolt

  • The revolt broke out mainly because: the Syrians rejected the French occupation, the spliting of Syria into states
  • In 1925, the Druze leaders (al-Atrash family) and the French high commissioner met because the Druze wanted the French general in their state to be replaced
  • The high commissioner rejected their position, threatened them with punishment, and antagonised the Al-Atrash family to diminish their influence
  • In 1925, these local grievances sparked the revolt to break out in the Jabal al-Druze State, led by Sultan al-Atrash
  • The revolt was an extension of the unrest that occured in 1920 after the French occupation of Syria started
  • The revolutionaries demanded a independent Syria and an end to the French occupation with the establishment of a Syrian National Congress
  • In October 1925, the rebels entered the city of Damascus resulting in a two day bombardment by the French
  • The revolt would continue until 1927, however the French had gained back some control by the end of 1925

The People's Party

  • The Druze rebels and nationalists of Damascus eventually joined together in the People's Party

End of revolution and Aftermath

  • In 1928, new elections were held for a Constituent Assembly
  • The Syrian nationalists won the elections and formed an assembly which drafter a constitution
  • However, the constitution was not accepted by the high commissioner due to Syrian unification and didn't include the French position of control
