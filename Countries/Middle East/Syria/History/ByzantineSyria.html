Byzantine Syria

  • The Byzantine empire was the eastern half of the Roman empire that survived after the western half fell in 395 BC
  • During Byzantine rule, Syria was administered from Constantinople (around the Sea of Azov)
  • During the 300s, Syria was subject to Persian invasions
  • In the 500s, the Persian invasions began again
  • In 540, Khosrow I, a Persian king, captured Antioch (northern Syria/southern Turkey)
