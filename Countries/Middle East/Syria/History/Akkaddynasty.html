Akkad dynasty

  • Akkad was an ancient region in modern central Iraq and ancient northwestern Babylonia
  • They were mostly Semitic and spoke Akkadian
  • Sargon founded Akkad in 2300 BC
  • He would go on to unite various Babylonian states and rule most of Mesopotamia including Syria, Anatolia, and Elam
  • After Sargon defeated Lugalzagisi, he gained rule over all of Sumer and brought him supremacy over all of south Mesopotamia and the Mediterranean coast
  • Sargons legacy his founding Mesopotamian minitary tradation and being the first great empire builder
  • His grandson, Naram-Sin, would eventually destroy Ebla bringing all of Syria under Akkad rule
  • The Akkad dynasty would eventually be overthrown by the Guti dynasty
