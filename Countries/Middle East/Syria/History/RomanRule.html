Roman Syria

  • In 64 BC, Pompey annexed Syria and left Judaea as a dependent state
  • In 55 BC, Pompey and Crassus were elected consuls and received command over Spain and Syria
  • Pompey reestablished many cities and reduced Judaea
  • Since 92 BC, the Parthians (from Iran) began invading northern Syria and the Seleucids
  • The Partians eventually signed a peace treaty with Rome
  • However, between 50-39 BC, the Partians continued to attack the Romans in Syria, with no avail
  • During the Partian invasions, Mark Antony, a general under Julius Ceasar, gave his spouse Cleopatra, Damascus, Ituraea, and Coele Syria
  • Under Roman rule, Syria was stretched northeast to the upper Euphrates by 73 BC
  • In 72, Commagene (northern Syria) was annexed to the Romans by Vespasian
  • In 6 CE, Judaea became a separate province and in 70 CE, Jerusalem was destroyed
  • In 177, Hadrian became emperor of Rome and took control over the Syrian army
  • Under his rule, he remaned the province Syria Palaestina
  • In 194, Roman emperor Septimius Severus, marched on the governor of Syria and took it over
  • Septimius would divide Syria into north (Syria Coele) and south (Syria Phoenice) Syria.
  • By 500, Roman Syria was divided into 5 different provinces
  • Under Roman rule, Syria's economy prospered due to natural resources
  • Under Roman rule, they also remained primarily rural and with the upper class taking on Greek culture while the lower class stayed Semitic
