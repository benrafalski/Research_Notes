Arab Conquests

  • During Byzantine rule, the Arabs also began to launch attacks on them (along with the Perians)
  • In 622, the prophet Muhammad started the Islamic conquests to spread Islam throughout the Middle East
  • In 633-634, led by Khalid al-Walid, the Islamic conquests began in Syria
  • In 635, Damascus fell to the Islamic army, the Syrians got to keep their property as long as they payed a poll tax
  • In 636, Heraclius staged a counter attack in the Arabs but was defeated
  • In 640, Syria was absorbed into the Caliphate
  • Syria was divided into four districts (junds): Damascus, Homs, Jordan, and Palestine
  • In 639, Muawiyah I (future founder of the Umayyad dynasty) was governor of Syria
  • He used Syria as a base to fight the Byzantines
  • Eventually, a Muslim civil war broke out in the Muslim empire after Ali was nominated as Caliph
  • Muawiyah had the backing of Syria and eventually won the civil war in 660
  • This established the Umayyad Caliphate, with Syria as the main base and Damascus as its capital
