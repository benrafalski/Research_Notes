Ayyubids

  • In 1173, Saladin led an army to defeat the Fatimid caliphate and united a Muslim front against the Crusaders from Egypt and Syria
  • In 1187, at the Battle of Hattin, Saladin defeated the Crusaders and recaptured Palestine for the Muslims
  • In 1193, Saladin died and the Ayyubid empire was divided among his family
  • The rule of Saladin and Nur al-Din before him saw great economic prosperity in Syria and they followed the Seljuqs in spreading Sunni Islam culture
  • Saladin aimed to destroy all Shia sects but was not completely successful, the Nazaris who lived in the mountains were a sect of Ismaili and maintianed some political importance
  • The kingdom was split between Aleppo, Hamah (Syria), Homs, Damascus, Baalbek (Lebanon), and Transjordan
  • In 1250, the Ayyubids were overthorwn in Egypt by the Mamluks and the dynasty fell in Syria by 1260
