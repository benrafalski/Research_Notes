Franco-Syrian Treaty of Independence

Dissolution of Contituent Assembly

  • In May 1930, the high commisioner dissolved the Contituent Assembly
  • He also enacted the Syrian nationalist proposed contitution with certain changes

Franco-Syrian treaty negotiations

  • From 1930-1936, Syrian-French negotiations were unfruitful
  • However, in September 1936 they were finally able to strike a deal, signing the Franco-Syrian treaty, due to the Popular Front government in France
  • The treaty gave Syria independence, Franco-Syrian consultation and foreign policy, and two French military bases in Syria
  • The Druze and Alawite states joined with the Syrian state to become one big state, Lebanon was not included

National Bloc

First Syrian Parliament

  • After the treaty was signed, the first Syrian Parliament was elected and Natioalist Bloc leader Hashim al-Atasi became president

Alexandretta Turkey claims

  • At the end of 1936 the Syrians ratified the treaty but the French never did
  • Turkey then made claims to Alexandretta and the French gave it to them in 1937 since they never signed the treaty
  • In 1939, it was officially incorporated into Turkey

Constitution suspended

  • In July 1939, the Syrian government resigned and the contitution was suspended when it became clear that the French were not going to ratify the treaty
