Ottoman Return

  • In 1840, the Eurpoeans signed the Treaty of London and forced the Egyptians to forfeit their rule back to the Ottomans
  • Following this, the Druze and Maronites fell into conflict in Lebanon
  • In Syria, the Ottomans tried a new system of taxation anc conscription but caused social unrest
  • Religious tensions also rose as the Muslim majority began to become aware of the European influence on the Christian and Jewish minorities in the region
  • This influence resulted in a flood of European goods into Syria from Christian and Jewish merchants
  • In 1860, the Druze-Maronite civil war in Lebanon caused a massacre of Christians by Muslims in Damascus
  • The Ottomans responded by sending a special commission to restore order and France sent a special force as well
  • The Europeans then concluded that Lebanon should become its own automonous region (mutasarrifiyyah) but left Syria as it was
  • From 1888, there were then 3 Ottoman provinces in Syria: Damascus, Aleppo, and Beirut
  • Around then, the French built a railway from Damascus to Beirut
  • In 1908, the Hejaz Railway was built to link Damasucus to Medina for the Mulsim pilgrimage
  • During this time, Aleppo had 200,000 people and Damascus had 250,000
  • These large cities saw a massive increase in Western culture from 1860-1910
  • This included middle/upper classes wearing western European clothing, Western schools and universities opening, but also Muslim schools for young men
