Hamdanid Dynasty

  • Hamdan ibn Hamdun rose to political prominence in his uprisings against the Abbasid caliphate
  • However, his sons would later serve as Abbasid officials
  • On son, Abu al-Hayja Abd Allah initiated Hamdanid leadership when he became governor of Mosul in 905
  • The dynasty became independent under Abd Allah's son, Nasir ad-Dawlah al-Hasan in 929
  • This is when the dynasty expanded westward from Iraq into northern Syria
  • In 945, Allepo and Homs were captured by Sayf ad-Dawlah
  • ad-Dawlah spent the rest of his rule battling the Byzantine Greeks and eventually lost Antioch in 969
  • In 979, the Hamdanids would be eventually driven from Mosul and their power shifted fully to northern Syria
  • Over the next few decades the Hamdanids would battle with their neighbors for control of Syria
  • Finally in 1002, control of Aleppo passed to a slave general Lulu before passing to the Fatimids
  • During Hamdanid rule, poetry and philosiphy flourished
