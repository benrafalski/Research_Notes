Abbasid Rule

  • After the Abbasids took over in 750, power shifted from Syria to Iraq
  • This made Syria a dependent state of the caliphate
  • During Abbasid rule, Umayyad sentiment still remained, until 905, when the last pro-Umayyad revolt was crushed
  • Under the Abbasids, Christians were not favored as much and Islamic conversion spread
  • Arabic also spread during this time, over Greek and Aramaic
  • Finally, in 877, Syria was annexed by the Tulunid dynasty in Egypt
