Perisan Rule

  • In 539 BC, under Cyrus the Great, the Achaemenian empire extended their rule from Iran/Persia and into Babylonia
  • Cyrus sent the Jews back to Jerusalem, who were captive in Babylonia, and encouraged them to rebuild their temple
  • After Cyrus, Darius I ruled the Persians
  • Darius was a great ruler and controlled Syria, Palestine, and Cyprus
  • During Perisan rule Damascus and the Phoenician cities were the centers of Syria
  • In 345 BC, there was a revolt in Sidon against Artaxerxes III
  • In 334 BC, Alexander the Great invaded Asia minor
  • In 333, under Darius III, the Persians were finally defeated by Alexander after he took Issus, Gaza, Tyre, and Persepolis.
