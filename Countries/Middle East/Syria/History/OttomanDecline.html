Ottoman Decline

  • During the late 1600s, there was widespread unrest in Ottoman territory but Ottoman rule remained stable
  • However, after 1700, the central government began to weaken in their administration and the Janissaries (elite army of the sultan) lost their order
  • This resulted in declining agricultural production due to falling tax collections and Bedouin attacks
  • The Ottoman authority revived somewhat in Damascus, ruled by the Azm family who was loyal to the sultan
  • The Azams reordered the Janissares and stopped Bedouin incursions
  • Ottoman authority was also revived in Sidon (Lebanon) by the Bosnian governor Ahmad al-Jazzar from 1775-1804
  • At the start of the 1800s, Alepppo and Damascus remained prosperous but most of the Ottoman empire continued to decline and the population was not able to be controlled anymore
  • In 1831, the Egyptians entered an army into Palestine and eventually controlled Syria and Asia Minor from the Ottomans
