Syrian Independence

World War II

  • In June 1940, the Franco-German armistice was signed and the French announced they would cease hostilities against the Germans and Italians and recognize the Vichy government
  • After the armistice, the Free French arose, led by General Charles de Gaulle, as the main military resistance against the Germans
  • In May 1941, the Vichy government let the Germans use Syria to refuel aircraft
  • However, by June 1941, British, Commonwealth, and Free French forces invaded the Vichy forces in Syria and occupied it by June 21
  • Until 1946, Syria was jointly occupied by British and French forces

Shukri al-Quwatli

  • Quwatli was orginially a member of the National Bloc in the early 1930s
  • By 1940, Quwatli became the leader of the Bloc due to his tolerance for corruption by other members of the Bloc
  • When Syria became independent in 1943, Quwatli was elected their first president
  • In 1947, Quwalti enacted a contitutional amendment removing one-term limits and he was reelected in 1948
  • In March 1949, Quwalti was overthrown in a military coup due to his failure in the Arab-Israli war in 1948 and overall disatisfaction with his rule

Social Unrest

  • The Franco-German armistice cause unrest to break out in Syria

Independence

  • In 1943, Syria finally became independent
  • With British help, by 1946 all French troops had left Syria
