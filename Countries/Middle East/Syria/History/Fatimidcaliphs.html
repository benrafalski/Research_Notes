Fatimid caliphs

  • In 935, the Ikhshidid dynasty was formed in Egypt and southern Syria but was short lived
  • By 969, the Fatimid dynasty from Cairo set out to expand west
  • Under the Fatimid caliph al-Muizz, the Fatimids conquered the Nile Valley, the Sinai, Palestine, and southern Syria
  • The Fatimids would later go on the take all of North Africa, Palestine, Syria, and West Saudi Arabia, Yemen
  • In 903, the Fatimids in Syria would begin to be threatened by the Qarmatians, an extreme Shia sect that rejected Fatimid rule, during an insurrection
  • In the early 1000s, the Seljuq turks began to attack the Fatimids and were eventually driven from Syria
  • After their attempt to oust the Abbasid caliph as leader of the Islamic world, in 1171 the Fatimid dynasty was finally ended
  • During the Fatimid rule, Islamic thought began to factionalize due to the Fatimids being Shia and the Abbasid caliphate being Sunni
