Sarawak ceded to Britain

  • After the Japanese occupation, Sarawak was devestated by war
  • After the Japanese occupation ended, Sarawak was eventually handed bck to Raja Vyneer Brooke
  • On July 1, 1946, Vyner terminated Brooke rule
  • He ceded Sarawak to Great Britain and made it a crown colony
  • This was proceeded by a bitter family feud and the native Sarawak population was not very happy with the deal and sparked an anti-cession movement
  • Many Malays also opposed the end of the Brooke Raja, resulting in political tensions for years to come
  • Groups for cessesion: Chinese, Iban, Indian
  • Groups against cessesion: Malay (Malay youth Association and Malay National Association)
  • In 1947, an extremist nationalist group called Rukun 13 was formed with Rosli Dhobi as its leader
  • In December 1949, Rosli Dhobi stabbed the second governor of Sarawak, Duncan Stewart.
  • Rosli was hanged and the Rukun was outlawed following this, all other anti-cessation movements were also banned
  • By February 1951, the anti-cessation movement was ended
  • The anti-cessation movement is seen as the beginning of Sarawak Malay nationalism and the fight for Sarawak independence
