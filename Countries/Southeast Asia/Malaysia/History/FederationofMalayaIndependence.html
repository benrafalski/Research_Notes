Federation of Malaya Independence

  • Following the signing of the London Treaty in 1956 at the Merdeka Conference, Malaya was on its way to independece
  • By August 1957, the Federation of Malay achieved independece under a government led by the Alliance
  • Reid Commission: was responsible for drafiting the Constitution of the Federation of Malaya
  • On August 31, Tunku Abdul Rahman read the Malayan Declaration of Independence at a ceremony in Kuala Lumpur at Merdeka Stadium
  • Merdeka day: Malayan indepencence say (August 31)
  • Kuala Lumpur was named the capital
  • Federation of Malaya Independence Act 1957: in July 1957, the British passed a bill in parliament giving the Federation independence
  • Aritcle 160 of the Constitution of Malaysia: part of the Malay constitution that defines what a Malay persion is (who can receive privileges under the Malay constitution), which includes: must be Islamic, must speak Malay, and must conform to Malay customs.
  • In August when they got independence, the Federation also joined the Commonwealth of Nations
  • Tunku Abdul Rahman become the first prime minister and also became the foreign minister
  • However, Sigapore, had its predominantly Chinese population and remained a British crown colony
  • The Malays remained the most powerful with the UMNO leading most federal and state positions and the Malayan sultans remaining
  • The Chinese were granted liberal citizenship rights however and remained economically powerful
  • UN Security Council Resolution 125: Adopted in September 1957, basically admitted the Federation of Malaya into the UN General Assembly
  • Anglo-Malayan Defence Agreement (ADMA): In September 1957, the British and newly formed Malayan Federation formed the AMDA as a bilateral defence agreement
  • States: Johor, Kedah, Kelantan, Negeri Sembilan, Pahang, Perak, Perlis, Selangor, Terengganu, Penang, and Malacca
