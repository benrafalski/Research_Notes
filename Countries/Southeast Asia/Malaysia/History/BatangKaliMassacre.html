Batang Kali Massacre

  • Killing during the Malayan Emergency
  • On Dec 12 1948, the Scots Guards killed 24 unarmed men in Batang Kali (northeastern Selangor) and then burned down the village
  • Notable for being the only massacre by the British during the war
  • After 1950, there were several British government investigations, however none of them have resulted in charges due to insufficient evidence to convict
  • In 1970, the British newspaper, the People, published testemonies from members of the platoon that the massacre happened
  • In 1990, UK officials pressured Malaysian authorities into halting a police inquiry into the massacre
  • The British position is that the villagers were killed as they tried to escape into the jungle, after being warned they would be shot if they tried
  • Members of the Scots Guard have claimed their was no escape attempt and they were ordered to carry out the massacre
