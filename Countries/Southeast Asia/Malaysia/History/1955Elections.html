1955 Elections

  • After the Malayan Emergency, negotations commenced following the British promise to grant Malayan independence
  • In 1952, the UNMO, MCA, and MIC then formed a new coalition, the Alliance party, with the UMNO as the leader
  • In July 1955, the negotiations finally led to the Federation of Malaya's first general elections
  • Previously, the legistature was made up of members appointed by the British High Commissioner
  • The elections were for 52 contested seats in hte Federal Legistalive Council (parliment for the Federation)
  • The Alliance party won 51/52 of the seats, defeating Parti Negara which was led by former UMNO president Onn Jaafar
  • The FLC would name Tunku Abdul Rahman Putra Alhaj as chief minister and home minister of Malaya
