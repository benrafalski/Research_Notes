Malaysian Indian Congress

  • In August 1946, the MIC was created by John Thivy to advocate for Indian indepencence from British rule in India
  • After India got their independence in 1947, the MIC turned to Malaya to advocate for Indian rights
  • They would become one of the founding members of the National Alliance (Barisan Nasional) in 1954, along with the UMNO and the MCA
  • Between 1951-55, Kundan Lal Devaser became president of the party and focues the party on Malayan independence
  • In the 1952 elections, the MIC lost to the Alliance, convincing them to join the Alliance in 1954
  • In 1955, the Malay Indian population became unsatisfied with Davaser for not advoating for better plantation rights for Indian workers and was forced to step down
  • This led to Tun VT Sambanthan becoming president and turning the MIC effectively into a Tamil party
  • Sambanthan tranformed the party into a conservative and traditionalist party emphasizing Indian culture/religion/language
  • In 1973, Sambanthan was forced to retire following an inner party rebellion, V. Manickavasagam took his place
  • In 1979, Samy Vellu became MIC president and served until 2010
  • During the elections from 1986-2004, the MIC had their most populatiy with between 6-9 general election seats
  • However, in 2008, they lost 6 seats and average about 3 seats in each of the following elections
  • Since 2019, the party is led by Vigneswaran Sanasee
