Sabah Self-government

  • Following WWII in 1946, North Borneo became a British crown colony
  • After the crown colony completed reconstruction, in August 1963, the British made Sabah a self-governing territory, similary to Sarawak
  • This gave North Borneo de facto self government

Early Political parties

  • United Pasok Momogun Organisation (UPMO): founded in January 1962 as a splinter group (in protest to the formation of Malaysia) of the UNKO by founder Orang Kaya Kaya to fight for the intrests of the Kadazan-Dusun-Murut races (later reunited with UNKO)
  • Sabah Chinese Association (SCA): Chinese political party formed in October 1962 as a merger between the United Party and Democratic Party with encouragement from the MCA of the Peninsula and was later dissolved in 1976
