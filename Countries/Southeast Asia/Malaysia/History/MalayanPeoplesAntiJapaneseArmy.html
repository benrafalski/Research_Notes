Malayan People's Anti-Japanese Army

  • After the invasion in December 1941, the MPAJA was formed
  • They were mostly ethnic Chinese guerrillas and pro-communist
  • The group was formed as a combined force of the Malayan Communist Party, British colonial government, and other smaller anti-Japanese groups
  • The MCP was seen as the leader of the group and Great Britain supplied them with officers and resources
  • The MPAJA never saw success against the Japanese however and the occupation continued until the end of the war
  • There was also a brief Chinese-led revolt in North Boreo during WWII against the Japanese too
  • The occupation led to increased tension between the Malayas and Chinese due to the selective treatment the Japanese engaged in, treating the Chinese worse
  • Furthermore, the occpation undermined the British authority in the region
  • The Brookes and the North Borneo company would be permanently undermined and anti-British sentiment among the Malay and Chinese in the peninsula would begin to rise
