Malaya Communist Party founded

  • In April 1930, the South Seas Communist Party formed into the Communist Prty of Malaya
  • The party was illegal under British rule
  • Members were mostly Chinese students in their 20's
  • Over the 1930's, they gained influence and organized various strikes
  • By 1939, they had over 40,000 members
  • After the Japanese invaded in 1941, the British accepted the MCP and the two allied to fight the Japanese
  • The MCP would go on to form the MPAJA and be the main anti-Japanese militant group in Malaya during the war
  • After the Japanese occupation ended, the British occupation started again and the MCP became anti-British again
  • By 1948, the MCP ws at the forefront of the Malayan Emergency against the British colonial government
  • The MCP formed the Malayan Peoples' Anti-British Army (MPABA) which turned into the 'Malayan National Liberation Army' (MNLA) to fight the British from the jungles
  • By 1955, the MCP began to negotiate with the government after being suppresses militarily
  • In 1957, Malaya became independet from Britain and in 1960 the emergency was declared over
  • In 1960, the US estimated that the MCP had about 2,000 members
  • In the early 1970s, the MCP only remained in Thailand and underwent many trials and executions after being accused of spying
  • This caused the formation of two splinter groups in 1970: the Communist Party of Malaya/Marxist-Leninist and the Communist Party of Malaya/Revolutionary Faction
  • The groups would eventually merge again in 1983 to form the Communist Party of Malaysia, which was dissolved in 1987
