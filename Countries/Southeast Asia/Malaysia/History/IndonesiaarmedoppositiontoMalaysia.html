Indonesia-Malaysia Confrontation (Konfrontasi)

Overview

  • After the creation of the state of Malaysia from the Federation of Malaya, Indonesia reacted with armed conflict due to their opposition to the Federation
  • Labuan was the headquarters for the Commonwealth (British associaiton) defense forces
  • Fighting occured between Indonesian, Malaysian, and Commonwealth forces (UK, Brunei, Australia, New Zealand), mostly along the land border in Borneo that East Malaysia and Indonesia shared
  • Indonesia was supported by China, Phillipines, the USSR, North Vietnam, and the Malayan Communist Party
  • Malaysia was supported by the US and Canada
  • Communist Chinese Sarawak insurgents also fought alongside the Indonesians due to their anti-cecession roots
  • Causualties: 114 soldiers killed and 36 civilians kiled (Malaysia side), 590 killed (Indonesian side)

Start of Conflict

  • In January 1963, Indonesia announced they would pursue a policy of Konfrontasi (non-compliance) with the British Malaysia proposal
  • The Indonesian president, Sukarno, thought Malaysia was a British puppet state and had also previously takes West New Guinea from the Dutch and looked to Malaysia next
  • At the Manila meeting, Indonesia agreed (along with the Phillipines) to let Malaysia form if most of the Sarawak and North Borneo residents wanted it
  • However, in September 1953, the Cobbold Commission found that most of the residents were in favor of joining Malaysia, Indonesia and the Phillipines rejected the results
  • Riots also broke out at the Malaysian and Singapore embassies in Indonesia
  • In April 1963, a police station was attacked by Indonesia forces for the start of the conflict

Operation Claret

  • In September 1963, Malaysia was officially formed and their troops entered the fighting
  • During this time peace negotiations began in Thailand and Japan, as well as direct secret talks started by Indonesian general Ahmad Yani due to worsening domestic conditions
  • By May 1964, Sukarno called for revolutions in Malays, Singapore, Sarawak, and Sabah and in August Indonesia expanded the air and sea battles to the Malaysian Peninsula
