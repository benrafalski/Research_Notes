Communist insurgency in Sarawak

Brunei Revolt

  • During the Japanese occupation of Sarawak, the Sarawak Anti-Fascist League was formed as a Communist movement against the Japanese
  • By 1956, the Sarawak Advanced Youths' Association was formed, absorbing many communist organizations in the area
  • In 1971, the North Kalimantan Communist Party (NKCP) was founded in Indonesia
  • All of these organizations were predominately Chinese and received little support from the Malays
  • The Sarawak Communist Organization (SCO) were anti-British colonialism and also against the formation of the Federation of Malaysia in 1963
  • In December 1962, the Sarawak insurgency broke out following the beginning of the Brunei Revolt
  • The Brunei Revolt was a failed coup against the Malaysian Federation by the North Kalimantan National Army, who wanted a state with Brunei, Sarawak, and North Borneo only
  • Following the Brunei Revolt, the SCO started a policy of armed insurgency in January 1963 and the British responded with anti-communist crackdowns

Malaysia-Indonesia Confrontation

  • During Konfrontasi, the SCO would fight with the Indonesians against the Malaysian/Commonwealth forces
  • The SCO used Indonesian Kalimantan as a base during the conflict and form the new North Kalamantan Communist Party guerrilla forces
  • The Indonesians themselves formed hte North Kalimantan People's Army with the help of the PKI in October 1965
  • In October 1965, General Suharto couped Sukarno in Indonesia and launched a purge on commnist elements in Indonesia
  • The Indonesian army would then begin to work with the Malaysian army to fight against the SCO insurgents, this was accompanied by a Dayak-led pogrom on the ethnic Indonesian Chinese in West Kalimantan

Goodsir Plan

  • In June 1965, the Sarawak and Malaysian Federal government impmeneted this plan as counter-insurgency
  • The plan resettled 7,500 people into 5 temporary settlements on the Kuching-Serian road in Sarawak
  • The villages were protected by barbed wire and resembed New Villages used during the Malayan Emergency
  • The Goodsir Plan succeeded (like New Villages), in cutting off the supply of food and supplies to the SCO from their Chinese supporters
  • By the end of 1965, there were three permanent settlements built to house 8,000 villagers

Conflict Resolution

  • In Febrary 1969, Tunku met with the SUPP from Sarawak in which the SUPP agreed to reverse their anti-Malaysian policy
  • In July 1970, the SUPP joined the Alliance Party, enabling the Malaysian Federal government to consolidate power over Sarawak
  • In March 1969, the communists were defeated in West Kalimantan and by July, a new North Kalimantan Peoples Guerrilla Force had emerged in their place after being formed by the SCO
  • The North Kalimantan Communist Party formed in 1970 fro the Sarawak Peoples Guerillas
  • In 1973-74, 75% of the NKCP agreed to surrender to the Malaysian government and in March 1974 the Sarawak governemnt and the communists signed the Sri Aman peace treaty
  • However, within a year about 100 communists returned to the jungle to continue the armed struggle
  • In 1989, the MCP and the Malaysian government signed the Hat Yai peace accords and the NKCP agreed to end their insurgency
  • In October 1990, the insurgency formally ended with the Wisma Bapa peace agreement
