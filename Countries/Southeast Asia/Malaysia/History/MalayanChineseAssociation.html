Malayan Chinese Association

  • In February 1949, the MCA was formed with the support of the British government
  • The MCA was founded in order to represent the Malaysian Chinese
  • The British wanted them to help manage the Chinese living in New Villages during the emergency
  • In 1951, the MCA was officially named a political party
  • Their first president was prominent Chiniese businessman Tan Cheng Lock
  • Their early supporters were mostly wealthy Chinese and they got little support from the New Villages
  • In 1955, the MCA was part of the Alliance Party
  • The 1955 elections resulted in the Alliance Party winning 51/52 seats
  • The MCA was dominant in elections until 1969 when they began to be challenged by the Democratic Action Party and Gerakan
  • In the 1969 elections, tensions between these parties rose so much they led to the May 13 Riots to break out
  • The 1969 elections resulted in a major loss for the MCA and severely decreased their status in the Alliance
  • In the 1995 election, Ling Liong Sik led the MCA to win 30/34 seats and 71/77 state seats
  • This success was also carried over in the 1999 elections and reestablished the MCA in the BN (former Alliance) coalition
  • Into the early 2000s, the party would be split among so called "Team A" and "Team B"
  • Team A was to be led by Ling's successor, Ong Ka Ting, and Team B was led by Chan Kong Choy
  • In 2004, the MCA united under Ong and Chan and won strongly with 31/40 seats and 76/90 state seats
  • However, in the 2008 elections, the MCA fared badly winning nly 15/32 seats, resulting in Ong leaving as president and being replaced by Ong Tee Keat
  • Over the later 2010s, the perty began to deteriorate and lose more support due to increase factionalism
  • By 2018, they only retained one seat in parliament and has remained a low status party ever since
  • The MCA controls The Star newspaper
