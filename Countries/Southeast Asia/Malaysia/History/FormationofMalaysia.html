Formation of Malaysia

  • In November 1961, the Malaysia Agreement was drafted that would give North Borneo, Sarawak, and Singapore to form the Federation of Malaysia
  • In January 1962, the Cobbold Commission, led by Lord Cobbold, was set up to determine of Sabah and Sarawak wanted to join the Federation of Malaysia
  • In August 1961, the Cobbold commision stated they recommended that Malaysia become a state due to their findings
  • Findings: 1/3 of the population of each territory strongly supported the formation of Malaysia, 1/3 supported it as long as their rights were protected, 1/6 wanted independence then Malaysia, and 1/6 wanted British rule
  • In July 1963, the Malaysia Act was passed in the UK parliament forming the Federation of Malaysia with North Borneo, Sarawak, Singapore, and Malaya
