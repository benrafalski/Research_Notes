Constitution

Overview

Background
  • Is the supreme law of the US, delinates the frame of the US federal government
  • Oldest and longest standing written and codified national constitution in force in the world
  • Articles of Confederation: first US constitution until 4 March 1789, when it was superseded by the US Constitution during the Constitutional Convention
  • Contains 7 total articles
    • Article I, II, III: embody the doctrine of the separation of powers
    • Article IV, V, VI: embody the concept of federalism, or laying out the states' rights and responsibilities
    • Article VII: outlines procedure for 13 states to ratify the Constitution
  • Amendments: allows for changes to me made to the Constitution, unlike other countries US amendments are appended to the end of the Constitution, there have been 27 amendments
  • Amendments 1-10: collectively called the Bill of Rights, order protections of individual liberty and justice while also placing restrictions on government power
  • Remaining 17 Amendments: mainly expand invididual civil rights protections

John F Kennedy Presidency

JFK Assassinated

Overview
  • Was assassinated while riding in presidental motorcade through Dealey Plaza in Dallas, Texas
  • Kennedy was in the vehicle with is wife Jacqueline, Texas Governor John Connally, and Connaly's wife Nellie
  • Kennedy was shot by Lee Harvey Oswald, former US Marine, from the nearby Texas School Book Depository 250 ft away
  • Kennedy was rushed to the Parkland Memorial Hospital and was pronounced dead 30 minutes after the shooting
  • Two hours after the assassination, Lyndon B Johnson was hastily sworn in as president aboard Air Force One
  • After the assassination, Owsald returned home and got a pistol that he used to kill Dallas policeman JD Tippit
  • Oswald was eventually apprehended by Dallas police about an hour after Kennedy and Connally were shot and was charged with the murders of Kennedy and Tippit
  • Two days later on 24 Nov 1963, a live TV camera showing Oswald being moved in the Dallas Police Headquarters captured Oswald being shot by Jack Ruby, Oswald died
  • Ruby was convicted of murdering Oswald, but was overturned on appeal, and Ruby died in prison in 1967 awaiting a new trial
  • The Warren commission spent 10 months investigating the murder and concluded Oswald assassinated Kennedy and that neither Oswald or Ruby were part of a conspiracy
  • In 1967, the New Orleans district attorney Jim Garrison brought the only trial for Kennedy's murder against Clay Shaw, who was aquitted
  • Further investigations also found that Oswald acted alone including the Rockefeller Commission and the Church Committee
  • However, in 1979, the US House Select Committee on Assassinations (HSCA) concluded that Kennedy was murdered as part of a conspiracy
  • The HSCA said there was a high probability there were two gunmen, based on a police Dictabelt recording that was later debunked by the US DOJ
  • For details on the assassination see JFK Assassination
