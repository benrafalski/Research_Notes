February 16, 2024

Summary of Story

  • Feb 13 South Africa lodges urgent request to ICJ to consider Rafah targeting as breach of orders
  • ICJ rejects South African request to impose urgent measures to safeguard Rafah
  • Also stressed Israel must respect earlier ICJ ruling from January 26

Summary of South Africa letter to ICJ

  • Sumarized Jan 26 ICJ ruling
  • Says Netanyahu has stated he wants Rafah evacuated
  • Netanyahu has said they will enter Rafah, mentions hostage rescue mission on 2/11
  • Rafah currently hosues 1.4 million, mostly in tents, ICRC says there is nowhere else for them to go
  • Many people say the chance of humanitarian disaster would greatly increase with a Rafah front
  • South Africa claims IDF in Rafah breaches the Genocide Convention and Jan 26 ruling
  • Says ICJ has power to make ruling without hearing
  • Wants ICJ to add additional provisions to Jan 26 order for Rafah

Israel response to South Africa letter

  • Reiterates rejection of original South Africa application from December
  • Israel is committed to international law
  • Says nothing in conflict has changed since Jan 26 order so no new provision is needed
  • Any military operation in Rafah is intended to target Hamas and required plans to protect civilians
  • Claims South Africa is weaponizing the ICJ
  • Says measures from Jan 26 already cover situation in Gaza as a whole, including Rafah
  • Wants ICJ to reject the South African request on basis of legal and factual merits

Summary of ICJ response to letter

  • ICJ says order from Jan 26 included all of Gaza, including Rafah
  • Thus, no modification to the original order is required
