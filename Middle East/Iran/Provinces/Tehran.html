Tehran

  • Tehran city is the capital of Iran
  • Population: 14 million
  • Largest city in Iran
  • At the foot of the Elburz mountains
  • Very young population
  • Hot arid climate
  • Challenged by pollution
  • Motahari mosque is in the center of the city
  • Also a miles long bazaar (market) in center of city
  • Core of city is surrounded by residential buildings
  • Massive immigration to Tehran in 1960-1990s
  • Most of the population is Shi'i
  • Economic center of Iran
  • Economy is dominated by the oil indistry
  • Traffic congestion is a major problem
  • City is divided into 22 districts, each with its own municipality
  • Larger Tehran city council as well, has some independence from central government
  • City struggles with water shortages
  • City has many paradise like gardens but has diminished in modern times
  • University of Tehran is main university and is publicly owned
  • Culture is dominated by Persian and Shi'i cultures
  • Iran's internationally recognized film industry is based in Tehran
  • Internet and blogs are extremely popular among younger population
  • Modern city with high-rise buildings
  • Milad tower, Azadi Tower, and Tabiat bridge are popular architectural sites
