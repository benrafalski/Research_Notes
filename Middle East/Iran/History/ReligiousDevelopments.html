Religious Developments

  • Under the Safavids the Shi'i clergy began to interfere with politics.
  • The ulama actively discouraged religions like Sufism and began to enforce Shi'ism among the masses.
  • During Safavid rule Iranian Shi’ism began to see a shift where Islamic legal conclusions rested with a group of qualified scholars called the mujtahid, rather than being interpreted from the legal writings of the 12 imams as it was before.
  • Once the Safavids collapsed, the role of Shi’i clergymen in political affairs only increased.
