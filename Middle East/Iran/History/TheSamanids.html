The Samanids

  • In 819, the Samanids began to gain power
  • In 875, they were given control of Transoxania (Uzbekistan and Tajikistan) by the Abbasid caliphate
  • In 900, Amr tried to overthrow the Samanids in Transoxania but failed
  • the Samanids, led by Isma il I ibn Ahmad, ended up overthrowing him instead and taking over Khorasan.
  • The caliph liked the Samanids because they spread Sunni Islam.
  • The Samanids are remembered for continuing to build Iranian nationalism, being prosperous, and laying the foundations for Iranian Islamic culture
  • They created New Persian, a Islamicate language that was a mix of Persian and Arabic.
