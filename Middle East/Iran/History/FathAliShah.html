Fath Ali Shah

  • Fath Ali's reign saw the start of strengthening diplomatic relations with the West and European rivalries over Iran.
  • He was defeated by Russia in two wars between 1804-1828
  • This lost him losing Georgia, Armenia, and Azerbaijan in the treaties of Golestan in 1813 and Turkmenchay in 1828
  • These treaties started a rivalry between imperial Britain and Russia over Iran.
  • In 1834 Fath Ali died and was replaced by his grandson Mohammand, who fell under Russian influence.
  • In 1848, Mohammad died and was replaced by his son Naser od-Din
