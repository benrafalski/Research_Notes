Seljuqs

  • In the early 1030's Mahmud permitted the Oguz Turks tribesmen to use Ghaznavid land for grazing grounds
  • Between 1038 and 1040 these tribesmen were united under the Seljuq family and drove the Ghaznavids out of Iran in the Battle of Dandanqan.
  • In 1055 Toghril Beg, the Seljuq sultan, invaded Baghdad, ended Buyid rule
  • This established Sunni Seljuq control over the Abbasid caliph along with the Seljuq dynasty
  • The Seljuq regime aimed to restore an orthodox Sunni Muslim political structure in Baghdad and suppress the Shiâ€™i Fatimid caliph who had gained power in northern Africa.
  • Over the next few decades, various Seljuq sultans would expand their territory and control all of Iran, Mesopotamia, Syria, and Palestine.
  • The Seljuq empire collapsed in 1153 after Turkmen tribes united under Atsiz captured the sultan because they did not want to pay taxes
