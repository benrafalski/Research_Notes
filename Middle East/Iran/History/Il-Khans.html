Il-Khans

  • Hulegu Khan, Genghis Khan's grandson, led the second Mongol invasion of Iran.
  • Since Genghis took Iran in 1231 there had been small dynasties fighting for power in the area
  • In 1256, Hulegu had captured all of Iran up to Baghdad and established the Il-Khanid dynasty
  • Halegu established his capital in Azerbaijan and set out to expand to the Mediterranean but was prevented by Egypt
  • After Hulegu took over, the Abbasid caliph was disbanded, sharia law was ended, and he established Mongol tribal law over Iran.
  • However, in 1295, when Mahmud Ghazan came to power and converted Iran back from Buddhism to Islam.
  • Ghazan's Islam was designed to appeal to both Sunni and Shi'i.
  • Eventually, infighting between Ghazan's military leaders led to the breakdown of the Il-Khans dynasty in 1335.
  • The Jalayirids took Azerbaijan in 1336 and the Muzaffarids took Yazd and Fars
