Assyrian empire in the west

  • In the 900-800 BC range, the Assyrians started to establish themselves in the west after a period of weak rule
  • In 853 BC, the battle of Karkar took place between the Assyrians and the Aramaeans/Israelites
  • The Assyrians were led by Shalmaneser III, the Aramaeans led by Ben-hadad I of Damascus, and Israelites led by King Ahab of Israel
  • The battle ended in stalemate
  • In 745 BC, Tiglath-pileser III took over following a rebellion and established an new and robust administration system
  • In 732, Damascus was finally captured by Tiglath-pileser III, uniting Assyria and Babylonia
  • However, military campaigns continued against the Aramaeans and Neo-Hittites for the rest of Assyrian rule
  • In 721 BC, Sargon II took over as Assyrian king, Sargon was presumed the son of Tiglath-pileser III
  • He was met with the challenges of dealing with the the Chaldean and Aramaean kingdoms in the south of Babylonia
  • By 720 BC, Sargon II took Karkar and made it an Assyrian province
