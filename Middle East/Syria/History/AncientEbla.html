Ancient Ebla

  • Ancient writings and cuneiform took place in southern Babylonia around 3000 BC
  • However, recent discoverys also suggest that clay tokens were also used for record keeping since 8000 BC before writing was invented
  • By 3000 BC, many Semetic people had arrived in Syria/Palestine/Babylonia.
  • The Elba Tall Mardikh palace south of Aleppo has more than 17,000 clay tablets from around 2600-2500BC
  • The Elba kingdom spoke Northwest Semitic
  • During their peak, the Elba controlled northern Syria, Lebanon, and parts of Mesopotamia (Iraq).
  • They enjoyed trade with Egypt, Iran and Sumer (Iraq)
  • The Elba were a prosperous kingdom due to their rich agricultral land
  • Their religion was polytheistic and primarily Canaanite
  • Eventually Elba came to the attention of Sargon of Akkad
  • The city was eventually burned and destroyed by an attack from Sargon's grandson Naram-Sin in 2240 BC
  • Elba was in decline for the next 250 years until an Amorite group ransacked the city and founded their own dynasty
  • However, many crafts and traditions of Elba culture remain in Syria today.
