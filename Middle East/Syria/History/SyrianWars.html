Syrian Wars

  • Series of 5 wars fought between the Ptolemies and the Seleucids, mainly over northern Syria
  • First war (274-271 BC): Ptolemy II gained Phenicia and most of Anatolia from the Seleucids
  • Second war (260-253 BC): Seleucid king Antiochus II regained Phoenicia and Anatolia from the Ptolemies
  • Third war (245-241 BC): Laodicean war, Ptolemy III started against Seleucid king Seleucus II where Seleucus had to concede territory in Anatolia and several coastal areas
  • Fourth war (219-217 BC): Antiochus Hierax started war then lost and conceded southern (Coele) Syria and Palestine to Ptolemy IV.
  • Fifth war (202-200 BC): Antiochus Hierax finally defeated the Ptolemies in Coele Syria and expanded Seleucid control over all of Syria
  • Antiochus then tried begin Helenization policies in Judea which led to a rebellion and Judea independence in 142 BC
  • After the years of contant war, the Hellenistic states (Seleucid and Ptolemy) fell under Roman rule during 100-50 BC
