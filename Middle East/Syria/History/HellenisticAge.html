Hellenistic Age

  • In 333 BC, Alexander the Great defeated the Persians and took over Syria
  • In 323, Alexander died and Syria was split between the Selucids from Iran (north) and the Ptolemies from Egypt (south) after the battle of Ipsus in 301 BC
  • Selucid and Ptolemy rule lasted for the next 100 years
  • The Ptolemies didn't disrupt the current population, while the Selucids founded many colonies
  • By 274 BC, Republics had replaced kings in many Phenician costal cities
  • In 200 BC, the Ptolemies were defeated by the Selucid king Antiochus III the Great and captured southern Syria
  • By 190 BC, Antiochus III was defeated the Romans and lost territory in Asia Minor (Turkey) leaving the Seleucids in decline
  • In 175 BC, Antiochus IV took over and began to spread Greek culture in Syria by urbanization and Hellenization
  • Under the subsequent Seleucid kings Syria would remain under contant civil war until Syria was made a Roman province in 64-63 BC
