Hittite Kings

  • Starting around 1600 BC the Hittite kings began to spread into northern Syria
  • The took the cities of Alalakh, Aleppo, and Ebla
  • Hatuslilis I was the earliest king to spread into northern Syria
  • Hatuslilis eventually designated his grandson Marsilis I as king in 1620 BC
  • Marsilis would go on to take Aleppo and Mari
  • He then ended the Amorite dynasty in the east in 1590 BC
  • Marsilis would die later that year and be suceeded by Telipinus
  • A power struggle then ensued and lawlessness ensued
  • He made the Edict of Telipinus and designated the pankus
  • The pankus was a general assembly that would be the high court for crimes
  • The edict was generally successful and crimes mostly subsided.
  • Later under Hattusilus III between 1275-1250 BC, the Hittites and Egyptians signed a defense pact and peace treaty
  • In 1193 BC however, the Hittite empire fell suddenly
